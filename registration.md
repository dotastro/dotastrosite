# How Registration Works

.Astronomy is a relatively small event with 60-100 participants. To ensure that we have diversity in expertise, background, seniority, gender, and representation from different parts of the world, we use an application process rather than open registration.

## The process

1. **Expression of interest:** When registration opens for an upcoming event, we publish an application form. You fill in your background, what you would bring to the conference, and ideas for sessions or a contributed talk.

2. **Review:** After the application deadline, the Scientific Organising Committee reviews all applications. We use [Entrofy](https://github.com/dhuppenkothen/entrofy), an open-source algorithm, to make a randomised selection based on diversity criteria set by the organisers -- including gender, career stage, geography, and whether you have attended before. This gives everyone a fair chance.

3. **Invitation:** Selected participants receive an invitation to register and pay the conference fee.

## What to know

- We typically receive 80-120 applications for 50-60 places, so the event is usually oversubscribed.
- Being turned down one year does not affect your chances the following year.
- If you have questions, email [info@dotastronomy.com](mailto:info@dotastronomy.com).

## Financial support

We aim to have funds available to support travel for a limited number of participants. You can express interest in this via the application form when it opens.
