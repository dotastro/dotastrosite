# .Astronomy α (DotDotAstro) — Online, 2020

**Date:** 17, 19, and 26 August 2020
**Location:** Online (synchronous sessions 14:00-21:00 UTC)
**Theme:** Virtual conferencing, accessibility, global participation
**Attendance:** ~75 people (max)
**Organisers:** Sarah Kendrew (ESA/STScI), Chris Lintott (Oxford), Robert Simpson (Google), Becky Smethurst (Oxford)

## About

The first online-only edition of .Astronomy, originally planned before the COVID-19 pandemic as an experiment in truly virtual conferencing. Styled as "..Astro" or ".Astronomy α", this event ran over three non-consecutive days in August 2020.

The conference featured the usual mix of talks, unconferencing, and a hack day, adapted for the online format. Key talks were recorded and edited in advance to avoid the "shouting into the void" feeling of remote presentations.

To preserve the small group feel of a normal .Astronomy, participation was capped at around 75. The event especially welcomed those who had been unable to attend in-person events due to travel constraints, family commitments, funding, or environmental concerns.

The three non-consecutive days were an experiment to reduce barriers to participation by not requiring three straight days of availability.
