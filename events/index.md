# .Astronomy Events

All .Astronomy conferences, from the first gathering in Cardiff in 2008 to the most recent edition in Madrid in 2024. .Astronomy was founded by Robert Simpson as a Cardiff PhD student, and has grown into a global series bringing together astronomers, educators, communicators, and developers.

---

## [.Astronomy 1 — Cardiff, UK, 2008](dotastro1_cardiff_2008/)

**Dates:** 22–24 September 2008  
**Venue:** Cardiff University Department of Physics and Astronomy  
**Organisers:** Robert Simpson  
**Hacks:** None (no Hack Day at the first event)

The founding event of .Astronomy, organised by Rob Simpson as a Cardiff PhD student. Featured live talks and remote presentations on blogging, open data, and Twitter for Telescopes. Formal proceedings were published by Canopus.

---

## [.Astronomy 2 — Leiden, Netherlands, 2009](dotastro2_leiden_2009/)

**Dates:** 30 November – 4 December 2009  
**Venue:** Lorentz Centre, Leiden University  
**Organisers:** Sarah Kendrew, Carolina Odman  
**Hacks:** First Hack Day

The first conference to follow the ".Astronomy format", including a Hack Day. Themes included citizen science, new media for outreach, virtual observatories, data mining, and visualisation tools like Google Sky and Worldwide Telescope.

---

## [.Astronomy 3 — Oxford, UK, 2011](dotastro3_oxford_2011/)

**Dates:** 4–8 April 2011  
**Venue:** New College, Oxford University  
**Organisers:** Robert Simpson, Chris Lintott, Arfon Smith  
**Hacks:** 1 documented (Chromotone)

Returned to Oxford with the expanded .Astronomy team. Known hacks include Chromotone, a multiwavelength astronomy sonification tool by Jon Yardley, Chris North, Haley Gomez, and Edward Gomez.

---

## [.Astronomy 4 — Heidelberg, Germany, 2012](dotastro4_heidelberg_2012/)

**Dates:** 9–11 July 2012  
**Venue:** Haus der Astronomie and Internationales Wissenschaftsforum Heidelberg (IWH)  
**Organisers:** Sarah Kendrew, Tom Robitaille, Markus Poessel  
**Hacks:** 7 documented

Hosted by MPIA in Heidelberg. Themes: visualisation, Javascript/Python in the browser, crowdsourcing, career development, hacking the literature, and global education. Hacks include AstroScanr, Flora, Zoonibot, and Science: It's Universal.

---

## [.Astronomy 5 — Cambridge, MA, USA, 2013](dotastro5_cambridge_2013/)

**Dates:** 16–18 September 2013  
**Venue:** New England Research and Development Center, Cambridge, Massachusetts  
**Organisers:** August Muench, Jenine Humber, Alyssa Goodman, Jose Galache, Louise Rubin, Sarah Block, Alberto Pepe  
**Hacks:** 3 documented (D3PO, Python ADS client, Don't Call Me Colin)

The first .Astronomy in North America. Hacks include D3PO (interactive publication-quality figures) and Andy Casey's Python client for the NASA ADS API.

---

## [.Astronomy 6 — Chicago, IL, USA, 2014](dotastro6_chicago_2014/)

**Dates:** 8–10 December 2014  
**Venue:** Adler Planetarium, Chicago, Illinois  
**Organisers:** Laura Trouille, Lucianne Walkowicz, Laura Whyte  
**Hacks:** 9 documented

Held at the iconic Adler Planetarium. Rob Simpson created BotAstro (a Twitter bot for astronomers) at this event. Other hacks include Cite Me Maybe, Kepler Song and Drum Machine, and Tones and Drones.

---

## [.Astronomy 7 — Sydney, Australia, 2015](dotastro7_sydney_2015/)

**Dates:** 3–6 November 2015  
**Venue:** Justice and Police Museum, Sydney CBD  
**Organisers:** Amanda Bauer, Arna Karick, James Allen, Vanessa Moss, Rob Hollow  
**Hacks:** 9 documented

First .Astronomy outside Europe and the USA. Introduced "Day Zero" tutorials, now a regular feature. Sarah Kendrew gave a public lecture on Giant Telescopes of the Future. Hacks include robo-ph, Space Shark, webcite, and Radio Yourself.

---

## [.Astronomy 8 — Oxford, UK, 2016](dotastro8_oxford_2016/)

**Dates:** 20–23 June 2016  
**Venue:** Pembroke College, Oxford University  
**Organisers:** Becky Smethurst, Chris Lintott, Sarah Kendrew, Edward Gomez, Ruth Angus, Mark Richardson, Sandor Kruk, Ellen Schallig, Sam Vaughan  
**Hacks:** 20+ documented

Returned to Oxford, organised by Becky Smethurst. Largest hack archive of any .Astronomy event. Hacks include The ArXiv Mailer (Dan Foreman-Mackey), AstroGoldStars, Hacks Collector, Fakespeare, Stellar Autopsies, and many more.

---

## [.Astronomy 9 — Cape Town, South Africa, 2017](dotastro9_capetown_2017/)

**Dates:** 14–17 November 2017  
**Venue:** South African Astronomical Observatory, Cape Town  
**Organisers:** Steve Crawford, Kevin Govender, Carolina Odman-Govender  
**Hacks:** 9 documented

First .Astronomy in Africa. Set against the backdrop of South Africa's growing astronomy scene (SKA, SALT, Office of Astronomy for Development). Hacks include Where in the Universe?, Whose Line Is It Anyway?, and Wave at Kepler.

---

## [.Astronomy X — Baltimore, MD, USA, 2018](dotastro10_baltimore_2018/)

**Dates:** 24–27 September 2018  
**Venue:** Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI), Baltimore, Maryland  
**Organisers:** Sarah Kendrew, Arfon Smith, Erik Tollerud, Joshua Peek, Ivelina Momcheva, Tom Donaldson, Susan Kassin  
**Hacks:** 10 documented

The tenth edition, styled as ".Astronomy X" (Roman numeral). Held at STScI, science operations centre for Hubble and JWST. Hacks include Data Sonification, Specreduce, a social justice reading group resource, and a public outreach tool for exploring research interactively.

## [.Astronomy alpha — Online, 2020](dotastro_alpha_online_2020/)

**Dates:** 17, 19, and 26 August 2020  
**Venue:** Online (synchronous parts 14:00–21:00 UTC)  
**Organisers:** Sarah Kendrew, Chris Lintott, Robert Simpson, Becky Smethurst  
**Hacks:** _To be documented_

The first fully online .Astronomy, planned before the COVID-19 pandemic as an experiment in virtual conferencing. Ran over three non-consecutive days to reduce participation barriers. Open to a maximum of around 75 participants to preserve the small-group feel.

---

## [.Astronomy 12 — New York, NY, USA, 2023](dotastro12_newyork_2023/)

**Dates:** 3–5 October 2023 (Day 0: 2 October; optional hacking: 6 October)  
**Venue:** Center for Computational Astrophysics (CCA), Flatiron Institute, New York  
**Organisers:** Kelle Cruz, Sarah Kendrew, Robert Simpson, Natalie Gosnell, Emily Hunt, Manodeep Sinha, Becky Smethurst  
**Hacks:** 4 GitHub repos documented (MOC hack, Astropy contribution session, Snakemake/showyourwork session, demo)

First .Astronomy since 2019 (after the online alpha edition). Held at the Flatiron Institute's CCA. Plenary talks were live-broadcast and recorded.

## [.Astronomy 13 — Madrid, Spain, 2024](dotastro13_madrid_2024/)

**Dates:** 23–26 April 2024  
**Venue:** European Space Astronomy Centre (ESAC), near Madrid, Spain  
**Organisers:** Emily Hunt, Sandor Kruk, Sarah Kendrew, Chris Lintott, Bruno Merin Martin, Jan Reerink, Robert Simpson, Becky Smethurst  
**Hacks:** 17 projects documented

First .Astronomy at a European Space Agency facility. 17 hack projects documented, including galaxy sonification, ESASky Virtual Assistant extensions, Astropy contribution sessions, and Astrotalks (arXiv for presentations).
