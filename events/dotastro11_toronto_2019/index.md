The 11th .Astronomy conference was hosted by the Dunlap Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics at the University of Toronto. This was the first time .Astronomy came to Canada, a country with a rich heritage in astronomy — including the development of Very Long Baseline Interferometry (VLBI), the discovery of Supernova 1987A, and observations of the first known black hole, Cygnus X-1.

Toronto has a large community of astronomers with interests across both astronomy and software/data issues, making it an ideal host for .Astronomy 11.
