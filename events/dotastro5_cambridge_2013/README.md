# .Astronomy 5 — Cambridge, MA, 2013

**Date:** September 2013
**Location:** Cambridge, MA, USA (Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics area)
**Theme:** Computing at scale, data mining, social media, citizen science, astronomy blogging
**Attendance:** ~60 people
**Organisers:** _Information to be added — submit a PR!_

## About

The fifth .Astronomy brought the conference to the United States for the first time, held in Cambridge, Massachusetts. The event continued the tradition of talks, unconference sessions, and hack days.

Invited talks included:
- A Smarter Zooniverse (Stuart Lynn, Zooniverse)
- Astronomy Blogging and Astrobites (Elisabeth Newton, Harvard)
- Crowdsourcing a High Dynamic-Range Image of the Entire Night Sky (David Hogg, NYU)
- The dotAstrophysics Data System (Alberto Accomazzi, SAO)
- An Experiment in Supporting the Public in Research and Publishing Results (Laura Trouille, Adler Planetarium / Northwestern)
- EyeWire (Amy Robinson)
- Communication Strategies (Amanda Bauer, AAO)
- The Fourth Paradigm: Data-Intensive Scientific Discovery (Tony Hey, Microsoft Research)
