# .Astronomy 1 — Cardiff, 2008

**Date:** September 2008
**Location:** Cardiff University, Astronomy and Physics Department, Cardiff, UK
**Theme:** Astronomy and the web: blogging, open data, citizen engagement
**Attendance:** ~30 people
**Organisers:** Robert Simpson

## About

The very first .Astronomy event, organised solely by Robert Simpson, then a PhD student at Cardiff University. This was the conference that started it all, bringing together astronomers interested in how the web could transform their science and outreach.

The event featured talks about blogging, hacking open data, Twitter for Telescopes, The Faulkes Telescope Project, and WETI (The Wait for Extraterrestrial Intelligence). Remote Skype presentations were given by Phil Plait (the Bad Astronomer) on blogging, Emily Lakdawalla (The Planetary Society) on public access to Solar System imagery, and Alberto Conti and Carol Christian (STScI) on Google Sky.

Formal proceedings were published by Canopus, though this approach was later abandoned as too complex and quickly out of date.

There was no Hack Day at this first event.
