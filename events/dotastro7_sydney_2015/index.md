.Astronomy 7 was held in Sydney, the first .Astronomy conference outside Europe and the United States. The conference brought the .Astronomy community to Australia, connecting with a vibrant local astronomy and technology scene.

This edition introduced ".Astronomy Day Zero", an optional day of introductory tutorials on commonly used tools to help participants feel more prepared for the Hack Day. This format continued in subsequent years.

## Programme

Speakers included:
- **Tom Robitaille**: Astropy and the open-source revolution in astronomy
- **Alice Williamson** (University of Sydney): Open Source Malaria. A New Way of Finding Medicines (cross-disciplinary talk)
- **Kirsten Gottschalk**: Astronomy education mini-websites
- **Nuria Lorente** (AAO): Software development from the perspectives of astronomers and engineers
- **Lisa Ballard**
- **Danny Vohl**
- **Yeshe Fenner**
- **Sze-leung Cheng**

A public lecture on Giant Telescopes of the Future was given by Sarah Kendrew at the University of Sydney during the conference.

## Hacks

[9 hacks documented](hacks.md)

Hacks from this event include:
- **Life Cycle of Astronomers** (Jennifer Piscionere): Visualising astronomy career paths with D3.js. [Source](https://github.com/jpiscionere/jpiscionere.github.io)
- **Friendly Virtual Radio Interferometer** (Cormac Purcell, Nuria Lorente, George Howitt, Andrew O'Brien, Demitri Muna): _Description to be added._
- **Literal Virtual Observatory** (Aidan Hotan): Drive around telescope observatories in a Mad Max/gamer-style interactive visualisation.
- **Pudding to the Max** (Vanessa Moss): Catch up with the exploits of the Cosmic Pudding at #dotastro 8 through Koalas to the Max. [Source](http://github.com/rhobslein/tothemax)
- **Radio Yourself** (Kirsten Gottschalk): Upload an image and see what you'd look like through a radio telescope. [Live](http://kirstengottschalk.github.io/)
- **robo-ph** (Tom Robitaille, Josh Peek, Katie Mack, Arna Karick): A podcast that reads astro-ph abstracts one by one. [Source](https://github.com/astrofrog/robo-ph)
- **Space Shark** (James Gilbert, Simon Mutch, Dany Vohl, Pascal Elahi, Kieran Leschinski, Kirsten Gottschalk): Alt-az pointer for education. [Source](http://github.com/spaceshark)
- **webcite** (Becky Smethurst, Lisa Ballard, Demitri Muna): Takes an ADS bibliographic code and plots a geographical web of citations for that paper. [Source](https://github.com/rjsmethurst/webcites_flask)
- **What's Up?** (Aaron Robotham): Updated Astroplan web app to help you plan observations. [Live](http://whatsup.icrar.org)
