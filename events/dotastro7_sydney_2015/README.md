# .Astronomy 7: Sydney, 2015

**Date:** 3-6 November 2015
**Location:** Justice & Police Museum, Sydney, Australia
**Theme:** Open source in astronomy, education, cross-disciplinary research
**Attendance:** ~60 people
**Organisers:** Amanda Bauer, Arna Karick, James Allen, Vanessa Moss, Robert Hollow

## About

.Astronomy 7 was the first .Astronomy conference location outside Europe and the United States, held at the Justice & Police Museum in Sydney.

This event introduced ".Astronomy Day Zero", a day of introductory tutorials on commonly used tools during the Hack Day. This optional day aimed to help participants who felt they needed more skills to fully participate in the Hack Day.

Featured speakers included Alice Williamson (University of Sydney) on Open Source Malaria, Tom Robitaille on Astropy and the open-source revolution in astronomy, Kirsten Gottschalk on astronomy education mini-websites, and Nuria Lorente (AAO) on software development from the perspectives of astronomers and engineers.

Full speaker list: Tom Robitaille, Lisa Ballard, Alice Williamson, Danny Vohl, Yeshe Fenner, Nuria Lorente, Sze-leung Cheng, Kirsten Gottschalk.

Sarah Kendrew gave a public lecture on Giant Telescopes of the Future at the University of Sydney during the conference.
