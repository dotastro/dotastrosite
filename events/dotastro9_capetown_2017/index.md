The ninth .Astronomy conference was held at the South African Astronomical Observatory (SAAO) in Cape Town, bringing the conference to Africa for the first time. The rapid growth of astronomy in South Africa, with major projects like the SKA, SALT, and the Office of Astronomy for Development, made it an exciting backdrop for a conference about advances in technology, software, and community.

The conference focused on how new technologies (machine learning, web interfaces, software development) could help astronomical research, education, and outreach, and how diversity could help us better understand the Universe.

## Hacks

[9 hacks documented](hacks.md)

Hacks from this event include:
- **Hack Graveyard** (Will Armentrout, Arna Karick, Lisa Ballard): Tracking hacks that didn't make it to completion, so they can be revived in the future. [Source](https://github.com/WillArmentrout/dotAstroGraveyard) | [Live](https://willarmentrout.github.io/dotAstroGraveyard/)
- **Hack Roulette** (Lisa Ballard, Daina Bouquin, Steve Crawford): Spin the wheel to generate a new hack using past dotastro hacks. [Source](https://github.com/dbouquin/hack_roulette)
- **Project PHaEDRA** (Charl Cater, Daina Bouquin): Rethinking a logo and website for Project PHaEDRA, a collaborative effort to digitize early astronomers' work.
- **Sketch dotAstro** (Daina Bouquin, Chris Lintott, Teresa de Young): Using computer vision to find sketches of Jupiter in archival notebooks. [Source](https://github.com/dbouquin/sketch_dotastro)
- **Wave At Kepler** (Ben Cook, Becky Smethurst): Static website to compute the direction to Kepler when it takes a photo. [Source](https://github.com/bacook17/hello-kepler)
- **Where in the Universe?** (Becky Smethurst, Brigitta Sipocz, Fernando Becerra, Lisa Ballard): Calculate your position in space if you were launched toward the zenith at birth. [Source](https://github.com/bsipocz/where-in-the-universe) | [Live](https://where-in-the-universe.herokuapp.com/)
- **Whose line is it anyway?** (Brett Morris, Nick Earl, Adrianna Pinska, Mike Walmsley, Demitri Muna, Becky Smethurst): A package for plotting common spectral line lists on your spectra. [Source](https://github.com/bmorris3/whoseline-vis/)
- **xorastro: Extremely Open, Extremely Reproducible Astronomy** (Joshua Peek, Orapeleng Mogawana, Brian Nord, Chris Lintott): A neural network to predict galaxy colors from galaxy shapes, built in the most open and reproducible way possible. [Source](https://github.com/jegpeek/xorastro)
