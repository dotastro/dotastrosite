# .Astronomy 2: Leiden, 2009

**Date:** 30 November to 4 December 2009
**Location:** Lorentz Center, Leiden University, Netherlands
**Theme:** Data-driven astronomy, citizen science, new media, visualisation
**Attendance:** ~50 people
**Organisers:** _Information to be added: submit a PR!_

## About

The second .Astronomy conference was the first to follow the ".Astronomy format" with a hack day. Hosted at the Lorentz Center at Leiden University, it explored how new technologies were changing astronomy for researchers and the public alike.

Key themes included citizen science (Galaxy Zoo), web-based platforms, new media for outreach (podcasting, blogging, microblogging), networked technologies for research (virtual observatory, literature tools, data mining), and visualisation (Google Sky, Microsoft WorldWide Telescope).

Invited speakers included Dr Michael Nielsen (author of Reinventing Discovery), Dr Arfon Smith (Oxford University), Dr John Taylor (Google), and Prof Andy Lawrence (Edinburgh University).

Sponsors included the Lorentz Center, Astron, the Royal Astronomical Society, the British Council, NWO, RadioNet, and Platform Beta Techniek.
