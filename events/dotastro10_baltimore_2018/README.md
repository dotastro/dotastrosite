# .Astronomy X: Baltimore, 2018

**Date:** 24-27 September 2018
**Location:** Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI), Baltimore, MD, USA
**Theme:** Ten years of .Astronomy, LSST and machine learning, ethics in AI, inclusion, cinematic astronomy
**Attendance:** ~70 people
**Organisers:** Sarah Kendrew (ESA/STScI, Chair), Arfon Smith (STScI, co-Chair), Tom Donaldson, Susan Kassin, Iva Momcheva, Josh Peek, Erik Tollerud (all STScI)

## About

The 10th edition of .Astronomy, styled as ".Astronomy X", was held at the Space Telescope Science Institute in Baltimore. This milestone event celebrated ten years of the conference series. Most talks were webcast live and recorded.

### Invited Speakers
- Andy Connolly (University of Washington): "Back To The Future: A Decade of LSST Development and the Growth in Machine Learning"
- Jarita Holbrook (University of the Western Cape)
- Sarah Horst (Johns Hopkins University)
- James Howison (University of Texas at Austin): "Software makes science better. But is it Research?"
- Alcione Mora (ESA): "Gaia: Science, Data and Challenges"

### Day Zero Talks
- Jane Rigby (NASA GSFC): "The Future of NASA Astrophysics Missions"
- Erik Tollerud (STScI): "Intro To Open Source/Astropy/Open Development"
- Clara Brasseur and Tom Donaldson (STScI): "Introductions to MAST, Astroquery.Mast Demo"
- Frank Summers and Alex Lockwood (STScI): "Cinematic Astronomy"
- Sarah Kendrew (ESA): "Ten Years of .Astronomy"

### Contributed Talks
- Stephanie Juneau (NOAO): "Galaxy Evolution at the Crossroads of Astronomy and Data Science"
- Ayat Mohammed (UT Austin): "Visualize This"
- Lauren Chambers (STScI): "A Different Kind of Dark Energy: Placing Race and Gender in Physics"
- Brian Nord (Fermilab/UChicago): "Ethics in AI"
- Dara Norman (NOAO): "Can Big Data Lead an Inclusion Revolution?"
- Craig Jones (STScI): "Hubble Image Discovery using Transfer Learning"

Sponsored by the American Astronomical Society.
