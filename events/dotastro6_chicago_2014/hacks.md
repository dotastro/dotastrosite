# Hacks from .Astronomy 6

.Astronomy 6 was held at the Adler Planetarium, Chicago, Illinois, 8–10 December 2014.

## Hacks

### AAS Job Viz
**Creator:** Vanessa Moss  
A visualisation of the AAS job register using location to pin jobs on a map. Allows users to filter jobs by type and location, using the Leaflet.js JavaScript library.  
[Live](http://www.physics.usyd.edu.au/~vmoss/jobvis)

### Adler Zodiac Story
**Creator:** Lucianne Walkowicz  
Online storytelling connecting the Chinese Zodiac to astronomy.

### Astronomer Bot (BotAstro)
**Creator:** Robert Simpson  
A Twitter bot for astronomers.  
[Live](https://twitter.com/botastro) | [Source](https://github.com/ttfnrob/botastro/)

### Cite Me Maybe
**Creator:** Andy Casey  
See which articles were referenced in your "field" that a paper did not cite.  
[Source](https://github.com/andycasey/cite-me-maybe)

### Elevate ApJ
**Creator:** James Davenport  
Working to elevate the readability and design of astronomy journal articles.  
[Source](https://github.com/jradavenport/elevate-apj)

### Kepler Song and Drum Machine
**Creator:** Ruth Angus  
Making music from Kepler light curves.

### Rate My Institution
**Creator:** Laura Trouille  
A way to honestly rate your place of academic work.

### Tones and Drones: Sampling Stars
**Creator:** Sascha Ishikawa  
Sonifying Kepler light curves to create music.  
[Listen](https://soundcloud.com/sascha-ishikawa/tones-drones-sampling-stars)

### Unclockify
**Creator:** Adrian Price-Whelan  
Chrome extension to find and replace sexagesimal coordinates on Wikipedia.
