## Hacks

[9 hacks documented](hacks.md)

Hacks from this event include:
- **AAS Job Viz** (Vanessa Moss): A visualisation of the AAS job register using location to pin jobs on a map, with filtering by type and location using Leaflet.js.
- **Adler Zodiac Story** (Lucianne Walkowicz): Online storytelling connecting the Chinese Zodiac to astronomy.
- **Astronomer Bot / BotAstro** (Robert Simpson): A Twitter bot for astronomers. [Live](https://twitter.com/botastro) | [Source](https://github.com/ttfnrob/botastro/)
- **Cite Me Maybe** (Andy Casey): Shows which articles were referenced in your "field" that a paper did not cite. [Source](https://github.com/andycasey/cite-me-maybe)
- **Elevate ApJ** (James Davenport): Working to elevate the readability and design of astronomy journal articles. [Source](https://github.com/jradavenport/elevate-apj)
- **Kepler Song and Drum Machine** (Ruth Angus): Making music from Kepler light curves.
- **Rate My Institution** (Laura Trouille): A way to honestly rate your place of academic work.
- **Tones and Drones: Sampling Stars** (Sascha Ishikawa): Sonifying Kepler light curves to create music. [Live](https://soundcloud.com/sascha-ishikawa/tones-drones-sampling-stars)
- **Unclockify** (Adrian Price-Whelan): Chrome extension to find and replace sexagesimal coordinates on Wikipedia.
